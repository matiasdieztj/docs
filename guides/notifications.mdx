Notifications keep you and your team in sync without requiring constant manual check-ins. Punto Diez delivers alerts both inside the app and to your inbox, and gives you precise control over which events trigger a notification so you can stay informed without being overwhelmed. This guide explains what notification types are available and how to configure them to match the way you work.

## Notification Types

Punto Diez sends notifications for the following event categories:

- **Mentions** — You receive a notification whenever a teammate uses `@yourname` in a task comment or project description. Mentions are designed to draw your attention to a specific conversation that needs your input.
- **Task assignments** — When someone assigns a task to you, you'll be notified immediately so you can review the details and get started.
- **Due date reminders** — Punto Diez sends automatic reminders **24 hours** and **1 hour** before any task assigned to you is due, giving you time to plan and prioritize.
- **Project updates** — Receive alerts when statuses change or new tasks are added in any project you follow. This is useful for staying aware of progress without being a direct assignee.
- **Team activity** — Get notified when new members join your workspace or when workspace-level changes are made, such as permission updates or plan changes.

## Configuring Notifications

You control exactly which notifications you receive and how they reach you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings > Notifications">
    Click your avatar in the bottom-left corner and select **Settings**. Then click **Notifications** in the left-hand settings menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review event categories">
    The Notifications settings page lists every event category — Mentions, Task assignments, Due date reminders, Project updates, and Team activity. Each row shows two toggles: one for **Email** and one for **In-App**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle email notifications">
    Switch the **Email** toggle on or off for each event type. When enabled, Punto Diez sends a dedicated email to your account address each time that event occurs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle in-app notifications">
    Switch the **In-App** toggle on or off for each event type. In-app notifications appear in the notification bell at the top of the Punto Diez interface in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your preferences">
    Changes are saved automatically as you toggle each setting. There is no separate save button required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## In-App Notifications

The **bell icon** in the top navigation bar is your hub for in-app notifications. A red badge on the icon indicates the number of unread alerts.

- **View notifications** — Click the bell to open the notification panel. Each item shows what happened, which project or task it relates to, and a timestamp.
- **Mark as read** — Click an individual notification to open the related task or comment, which marks it as read automatically. To mark all notifications as read at once, click **Mark all as read** at the top of the panel.
- **Filter by workspace** — If you belong to multiple workspaces, use the workspace filter at the top of the notification panel to view alerts from a specific workspace only.

## Email Digest

Instead of receiving individual emails for every event, you can opt for a consolidated summary delivered on a schedule.

To enable the email digest, go to **Settings** → **Notifications** and scroll to the **Email Digest** section. Choose either:

- **Daily digest** — a summary email sent each morning covering all activity from the previous day.
- **Weekly digest** — a summary email sent every Monday morning covering activity from the past week.

When the digest is enabled for a notification type, Punto Diez will batch those alerts into the digest instead of sending them individually.

<Tip>
  Use **@mentions** in task comments to loop in a specific teammate on a conversation. Mentions cut through the noise — your teammate will receive a direct notification regardless of whether they're following the project, making them the most reliable way to get someone's attention quickly.
</Tip>
