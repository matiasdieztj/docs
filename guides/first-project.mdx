Projects are the core building blocks of Punto Diez. Each project gives you a dedicated space to organize tasks, assign work to teammates, and monitor progress — all from a single, unified view. This guide walks you through setting up your first project from scratch and getting your team moving.

## Creating a Project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your workspace">
    In the left sidebar, click on your workspace name to expand the navigation. All your existing projects and shared spaces are listed here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Project">
    In the top-right corner of the workspace view, click the **New Project** button. A creation dialog will appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in project details">
    Enter a **project name** and an optional **description** to help teammates understand the project's purpose. You can also set an optional **due date** to mark the project's target completion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a view">
    Select the default view for your project:
    - **List** — a structured, line-by-line view of all tasks.
    - **Board** — a Kanban-style column layout grouped by status.
    - **Timeline** — a Gantt-style view for planning across dates.

    You can switch between views at any time after the project is created.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Project">
    Click the **Create Project** button to finalize. Punto Diez will open your new project immediately, ready for you to add tasks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Tasks

Once your project is created, populate it with tasks to represent the individual units of work your team needs to complete.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the task creation panel">
    Inside your project, click the **+ Add Task** button at the bottom of any list or column. A new task row will appear inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a task name">
    Type a descriptive name for the task and press **Enter** to confirm, or click the task to open its detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the task">
    In the task detail panel, click the **Assignee** field and select one or more teammates from the dropdown. Assigned members will receive a notification automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a priority">
    Click the **Priority** field and choose from:
    - **Low** — nice to have, no immediate deadline pressure.
    - **Medium** — standard work with a moderate timeline.
    - **High** — important work that should be completed soon.
    - **Urgent** — blocking or time-critical; requires immediate attention.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a due date">
    Click the **Due Date** field and select a date from the calendar picker. Tasks with due dates appear in Timeline view and trigger due-date reminder notifications.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Task Statuses

Every task in Punto Diez moves through a series of statuses that reflect its current state. By default, each project includes four statuses:

- **To Do** — the task has been created but work hasn't started.
- **In Progress** — a teammate is actively working on the task.
- **In Review** — the work is complete and awaiting review or approval.
- **Done** — the task has been reviewed and closed out.

To customize statuses for your project, open **Project Settings** by clicking the `···` menu next to the project name in the sidebar, then select **Statuses**. From there you can rename existing statuses, change their colors, reorder them, or add new ones to match your team's workflow.

## Tracking Progress

Punto Diez gives you several tools to keep a pulse on how a project is advancing.

**Progress bar** — At the top of every project, a visual progress bar shows the percentage of tasks marked as **Done** relative to the total task count. The bar updates in real time as tasks are completed.

**Filtering tasks** — Use the **Filter** toolbar above the task list to narrow tasks by:
- **Status** — show only tasks in a specific stage.
- **Assignee** — focus on one person's workload.
- **Label** — group related tasks by custom labels you define.

**Activity log** — Click the **Activity** tab inside your project to see a chronological record of every change made — task creation, status updates, assignee changes, comments, and more. Use this log to audit progress or catch up after time away.

<Tip>
  Press **N** anywhere inside a project to instantly open a new task. This keyboard shortcut works in List, Board, and Timeline views, letting you capture work quickly without reaching for the mouse.
</Tip>
