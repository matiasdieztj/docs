Workspaces are the primary way you separate different teams, departments, or areas of work inside your Punto Diez organization. Each workspace has its own projects, members, and settings, so you can tailor the environment to the specific group using it — without any cross-contamination between teams. Whether you're running a small startup or a large enterprise, workspaces give you the structure to keep every team focused and organized.

## Creating a Workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Workspaces">
    In the left-hand sidebar, click **Workspaces** to open the workspaces panel. You'll see a list of all workspaces you currently belong to within your organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Workspace">
    Click the **+ New Workspace** button in the top-right corner of the workspaces panel. A creation dialog will appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a Name and Description">
    Type a clear, descriptive **name** for your workspace (e.g., "Engineering", "Marketing", "Client: Acme Corp"). Add an optional **description** to help members understand the workspace's purpose.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Visibility">
    Select a visibility setting:
    - **Public** — any member of the organization can find and join this workspace.
    - **Private** — the workspace is hidden from the organization directory; only invited members can access it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create">
    Click **Create Workspace**. Your new workspace is ready immediately. You'll be taken directly into it, where you can start adding projects and inviting members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Workspace Roles

Every member of a workspace is assigned a role that controls what they can see and do. Roles are set at the workspace level, so the same user can have different permissions in different workspaces.

| Role    | Permissions |
|---------|-------------|
| **Owner** | Full control over the workspace, including billing settings, member management, and the ability to delete the workspace. |
| **Admin** | Manage workspace members and their roles; create, archive, and delete projects. Cannot access billing settings. |
| **Member** | Create and edit projects and tasks within the workspace. Cannot manage other members or workspace settings. |
| **Guest** | View-only access to projects and tasks they've been explicitly granted access to. Cannot create or edit any content. |

<Tip>
  Assign the **Guest** role to external stakeholders or clients who need visibility into progress without the ability to make changes. This keeps your data safe while enabling transparent collaboration.
</Tip>

## Switching Workspaces

To move between workspaces, click the **workspace selector** in the top-left corner of the dashboard — it displays the name of your currently active workspace. A dropdown will appear listing all workspaces you belong to within the current organization.

Click any workspace name to switch to it instantly. The sidebar, projects, and activity feed will all update to reflect the selected workspace.

If you belong to multiple organizations, switch organizations first using the **organization menu** in your profile, then select the workspace you need.

## Workspace Settings

Workspace Owners and Admins can manage workspace settings by navigating to **Workspace Settings** from the sidebar.

From this page you can:

- **Rename the workspace** — update the name to reflect a team restructure or project realignment.
- **Change the description** — keep the purpose of the workspace clear for current and future members.
- **Update visibility** — switch between Public and Private as your needs change.
- **Archive the workspace** — freeze the workspace in a read-only state. Archived workspaces and their contents are preserved but no new work can be added.
- **Delete the workspace** — permanently remove the workspace and all of its contents.

<Warning>
  Deleting a workspace is **irreversible**. All projects, tasks, comments, and attachments inside the workspace will be permanently and immediately deleted. There is no way to recover this data after deletion. Archive the workspace instead if you may need to reference its contents in the future.
</Warning>
