After creating your Punto Diez account, take a few minutes to configure your profile and security settings. A properly set-up account keeps your data secure, ensures you receive the right notifications, and gives you the API access you need to work efficiently.

## Profile Settings

Your profile information is visible to teammates in your shared workspaces. Keep it accurate so collaborators can identify and reach you.

To update your profile, go to **Settings → Profile** and edit any of the following fields:

- **Full name** — your display name across all workspaces.
- **Email address** — used for login and notifications. Changing your email requires re-verification.
- **Avatar** — upload a photo or image to personalize your account.
- **Timezone** — set your local timezone so due dates and timestamps display correctly for you.

Click **Save Changes** after updating any field.

## Security

Protecting your account starts with a strong password and an extra layer of verification. Punto Diez supports both password management and two-factor authentication (2FA).

### Change Your Password

Go to **Settings → Security** and click **Change Password**. Enter your current password, then enter and confirm your new one. Click **Update Password** to save.

### Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second verification step each time you log in. Follow these steps to enable it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security settings">
    Go to **Settings → Security** and locate the **Two-Factor Authentication** section. Click **Enable 2FA**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your preferred authenticator app — such as Google Authenticator or Authy — and scan the QR code displayed on screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the verification code">
    Type the 6-digit code generated by your authenticator app into the confirmation field on the setup page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your backup codes">
    Punto Diez displays a set of one-time backup codes. Save these in a secure location. Use them to access your account if you lose access to your authenticator app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm activation">
    Click **Confirm & Enable**. Two-factor authentication is now active on your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Manage Active Sessions

Under **Settings → Security → Active Sessions**, you can see every device and browser currently signed into your account. Click **Revoke** next to any session you don't recognize to sign it out immediately.

## API Keys

API keys let you authenticate requests to the Punto Diez REST API from your own applications, scripts, or integrations.

To generate an API key, go to **Settings → API Keys** and click **New API Key**. Give the key a descriptive name — for example, `production-integration` or `local-dev` — so you can identify its purpose later.

<Warning>
  Your API key is only shown once, immediately after creation. Copy it and store it in a secure location such as a password manager or environment variable. If you lose it, you must revoke the key and generate a new one.
</Warning>

To revoke a key, find it in the list under **Settings → API Keys** and click **Revoke**. Revoked keys stop working immediately and cannot be restored.

## Notification Preferences

Control which events trigger email or in-app notifications so you stay informed without being overwhelmed.

Go to **Settings → Notifications** to customize your preferences. You can toggle the following independently for both email and in-app channels:

- **Task assignments** — notify you when a task is assigned to you.
- **Task comments** — notify you when someone comments on a task you're involved in.
- **Project updates** — notify you when a project's status or details change.
- **Team invitations** — notify you when someone joins your workspace.
- **Billing alerts** — notify you of payment activity, plan changes, or usage limits.

Toggle each option on or off and click **Save Preferences** to apply your changes.
